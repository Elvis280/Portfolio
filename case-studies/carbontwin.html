02 Problem Statement

How might we empower city planners to test and compare CO‚ÇÇ capture strategies before committing resources, using data-driven simulations that predict real-world impact?

Urban carbon management requires evidence-based decision-making:

  • High implementation costs: Installing capture infrastructure (biofilters, roadside units) requires significant investment without guaranteed ROI
  • Uncertainty in effectiveness: Without modeling, it's unclear which intervention will yield the best CO‚ÇÇ reduction for a specific urban layout
  • Complex geospatial factors: Wind patterns, emission sources, and population density interact in unpredictable ways
  • Need for scenario testing: Decision-makers need a safe sandbox to experiment with "what-if" questions before deployment

11 Key Learnings

üí°

Geospatial Visualization is Key

Early prototypes used only charts. Adding the Leaflet.js map with color-coded CO‚ÇÇ hotspots made data instantly understandable. Stakeholders engaged more with spatial context than raw numbers.

üéØ

KPIs Drive Decisions

City planners needed quantitative proof for budget approvals. Showing "35% CO‚ÇÇ reduction" was more persuasive than qualitative descriptions. Data-driven KPIs justified infrastructure investments.

üì±

Glassmorphism Enhances Credibility

The modern glassmorphism UI with holographic effects made presentations feel professional and cutting-edge. Stakeholders perceived the tool as more trustworthy than traditional spreadsheet models.

üîÑ

Dual Themes for Different Contexts

Tech Mode (dark/cyan) worked better for technical presentations, while Nature Mode (light/mint green) suited public outreach. Theme flexibility addressed diverse stakeholder needs.
