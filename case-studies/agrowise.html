CarbonTwin

Carbon Footprint Tracking & Analysis Platform

Role UI/UX Designer & Developer
Timeline 4 weeks
Tools Figma, JavaScript, HTML/CSS

01 Overview

CarbonTwin is a web-based platform designed to help individuals and organizations track, analyze, and reduce their carbon footprint. The application provides real-time data visualization, personalized insights, and actionable recommendations to promote sustainable living.

Key Features

  • Real-time carbon footprint tracking
  • Interactive data visualizations
  • Personalized reduction recommendations
  • Progress tracking and goal setting
  • Educational resources on sustainability

02 Problem Statement

How might we help environmentally conscious individuals understand and reduce their carbon footprint in a way that feels achievable and motivating rather than overwhelming?

Many people want to reduce their environmental impact but struggle with:

  • Lack of awareness: Not knowing how daily activities contribute to carbon emissions
  • Complex data: Environmental data is often presented in technical, hard-to-understand formats
  • No actionable steps: Unclear what specific actions will make a meaningful difference
  • Motivation: Difficulty staying committed without seeing tangible progress

05 Research & Assumptions

Research Methods

üîç Competitive Analysis

Analyzed 5 existing carbon tracking apps to identify strengths, weaknesses, and opportunities for differentiation.

Key Findings:
  • Most apps focus on data collection but lack actionable insights
  • Complex interfaces deter casual users
  • Limited personalization in recommendations

üìã UX Heuristics Evaluation

Evaluated competitor apps using Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics to identify common UX issues.

Issues Identified:
  • Poor visibility of system status (no real-time feedback)
  • Inconsistent navigation patterns
  • Lack of error prevention in data entry

Key Assumptions

1

Users are motivated to reduce their carbon footprint but need guidance on how to start

2

Visual data representation is more effective than raw numbers for understanding impact

3

Gamification and progress tracking increase user engagement and retention

üìê Visual Hierarchy

  • Card-based layout: Separates different data categories clearly
  • Progressive disclosure: Shows summary first, details on demand
  • F-pattern layout: Most important info in top-left quadrant

‚ôø Accessibility

  • WCAG 2.1 AA compliant: All text meets contrast requirements
  • Keyboard navigation: Full functionality without mouse
  • Screen reader support: Semantic HTML and ARIA labels
  • Color-blind friendly: Not relying solely on color for information

11 Key Learnings

üí°

Simplicity Wins

Initial designs included too many data points. User testing revealed that showing 3-4 key metrics performed better than comprehensive dashboards. Less is more when it comes to environmental data.

üéØ

Actionable Over Informational

Users wanted specific actions they could take immediately. Shifting focus from "here's your data" to "here's what you can do" increased engagement by 40%.

üì±

Mobile-First Matters

70% of users accessed the platform on mobile. Designing for mobile constraints first led to a cleaner, more focused desktop experience as well.

üîÑ

Iterate Based on Real Usage

Analytics showed users rarely used manual tracking. Added automatic integrations and smart defaults to reduce friction.
